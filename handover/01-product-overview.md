# 01 — Product Overview

## The problem

Most of us walk around with a head full of open loops. A deadline. An email we
haven't replied to. A worry about a family member. A half-formed idea we don't
want to lose. None of it is written down, so the brain keeps *re-surfacing* each
one to make sure we don't forget — which is exhausting, scatters our focus, and
quietly raises our stress all day.

Productivity apps mostly make this worse. They're dense, they nag, and they treat
you like a machine that should be doing more. What people actually need is the
opposite: a way to **put it all down**, feel lighter, and deal with one thing at a
time.

## What Headroom is

Headroom is a calm, single-purpose web app that externalises your mental load and
helps you reduce it.

You type out whatever is on your mind. Each thought appears as a **glowing neuron
on a beautiful, living image of a brain** — grouped by what kind of thought it is,
sized by how big it feels, and glowing red-hot if it's a worry keeping you up. The
fuller your head, the more the brain lights up. As you deal with things and set
them down, the brain quietens and your "headroom" — the mental space you have free
— goes up.

It's part journal, part to-do list, part focus timer, and part gentle coach — but
the feeling it's going for is closer to *taking a breath* than *getting organised*.

## Who it's for

The starting audience is **knowledge workers** — people whose job is thinking, who
juggle many threads at once and feel the cost of it. But the underlying need
(too much in my head, help me set it down) is close to universal, especially for
people who describe themselves as easily overwhelmed or as having a busy,
racing mind.

## The philosophy — why it feels different

Three principles run through every design decision:

1. **Calm is the product.** The app itself must lower tension, not add to it.
   Small, cramped text reads as anxious, so the type is large and spacious. The
   brain is always the hero — nothing clutters it. Landing on the site should
   feel like exhaling.

2. **One thing at a time.** Overloaded people can't triage a wall of tasks. So the
   app is relentless about surfacing *the single most important thing*, and the
   Focus screen is built around giving *one* thought your whole attention.

3. **Honesty over judgement.** Headroom tells you the truth gently — how your head
   feels, whether a focus session really was focused — but it never shames you or
   spies on you. Where other tools surveil, Headroom asks.

## The three screens at a glance

- **Brain dump** (the home) — empty your head; see it as neurons on the brain.
- **Focus** — give one thing undivided attention, with breaks and an honest recap.
- **Overview** — your mental state and your day, at a glance.

Each is explained in full in [02 — Feature Guide](02-feature-guide.md).

## Where the AI fits

An optional AI coach (powered by Anthropic's Claude) adds four gentle helpers:
reframing a worry so it stops circling, breaking a big task into small steps,
sorting a messy brain-dump into tidy thoughts, and answering questions about your
own list ("what should I tackle first?"). Crucially, **the app works fully without
it** — if the AI is switched off, everything still functions with sensible
built-in responses.
