# 03 — A Day With Headroom

A narrative walkthrough, so you can picture how someone actually lives with the
app. Meet a user starting a busy Monday.

---

**8:40am — arriving.**
She opens the site. A short calming loading moment plays, then the brain glows into
view with a quiet greeting: *"Morning. 62% headroom · 5 open · 1 worry."* Nothing
is demanding anything of her. She takes a breath.

**8:41am — emptying her head.**
Everything that's been circling since she woke up, she types out one line at a
time: *finish the board deck, reply to the investor email, will we make payroll,
book the dentist, that newsletter idea.* Each becomes a neuron. The payroll worry
pulses red. Her head is now on the screen instead of in her chest — and it already
feels lighter.

**8:43am — too much at once.**
Actually there's more, and it's tangled. Instead of sorting it, she hits **Sweep
my head**, pours out a messy paragraph, and the app hands back six tidy, sorted
thoughts. She taps to accept them all.

**8:45am — what matters?**
She taps **Plan my day**. The coach reads her list and tells her: start with the
board deck (open it, don't finish it — just the first five minutes), then the
investor email; the newsletter idea can wait; and she has permission not to
carry the payroll worry all day, because there's nothing to do about it before
Thursday.

**9:00am — doing one thing.**
She goes to **Focus**, picks "finish the board deck," sets 45 minutes, and starts.
The ring begins to fill. For a while she's in it.

**9:26am — the wobble.**
A Slack ping pulls her into another tab. Fifteen minutes later, a soft notification:
*"Still on task? — is this part of it?"* She comes back. Headroom asks whether the
detour was part of the task. It wasn't — she taps **I drifted.**

**9:52am — the honest recap.**
She finishes the round. The recap doesn't flatter her: *28m true focus · −15m
adrift · 1 round.* It also notes she jotted down two new thoughts mid-session. She
rates how her head feels — a 4 — and marks the board deck **done**. On the brain,
that neuron settles and dims. Headroom ticks up.

**11:30am — the reset.**
Her head has filled again and it's been a while since she cleared it. A gentle
prompt appears: *"Ready to set something down?"* She takes the two-minute breathing
reset. The ring breathes in and out; a single calming line sits underneath. When it
ends, it asks how she feels and points her at the one thing worth picking up next.

**5:30pm — closing the day.**
She opens **Overview**. Three cards tell her where she stands. The trend line shows
her headroom climbing across the week, with the "how it felt" dots tracking close
behind. The quiet-sessions timeline shows the three focused blocks she managed
today. She can see, plainly, that she did the things that mattered — and her head
is measurably clearer than it was at 8:40.

---

## The loop, in one sentence

**Empty your head → let the app help you pick the one thing → focus on it properly
→ watch it add up.** That's the whole product, and every feature serves that loop.
