# 05 — Glossary

Every Headroom term, one line each. Keep this handy the first week.

---

**Headroom** — The product name, and also the core metric: how much *mental space*
you have free. High headroom = clear head; low headroom = overloaded. Shown as a
percentage.

**Brain dump** — The home screen, and the act of emptying your head into it. Each
thing you type becomes a neuron on the brain.

**Neuron** — A single thought shown as a glowing point of light on the brain image.
Its colour is its category, its size is how big the thought is, its glow is its
urgency.

**Mental load** — How full your head is right now (Clear / Light / Busy / Heavy),
based on how many open thoughts you have and how urgent they are.

**The categories** — The five kinds of thought:
- **Task** (blue) — work needing focused effort
- **To-do** (teal) — an errand or bit of life admin
- **Keeping me up** (red) — a worry that loops
- **Idea** (purple) — something to explore or make
- **Goal** (amber) — a longer-term aspiration with a target date

**Urgency** — How soon a thought matters (P1–P3). Drives how brightly its neuron
glows and pulses. Separate from size.

**Size** — How big a thought feels (Small / Medium / Large). Drives how large its
neuron is. Separate from urgency.

**Set it down** — Headroom's phrase for dealing with a thought and letting it go —
either by marking it done or by having the coach help you offload the worry.

**Running hot** — The category carrying the most stress right now, flagged at the
top of the panel with an offer to take a reset.

**The Coach** — The gentle prompt that appears when you're overloaded, offering a
two-minute breathing reset. It never nags.

**Reset** — A two-minute guided breathing pause (a ring that breathes in and out),
followed by a "how does your head feel?" rating.

**Felt rating** — Your own 1–10 answer to "how does your head feel?" after a reset
or focus session. Lets the app compare how clear your head *actually feels* against
what it *computes*.

**Quiet session / Focus session** — A stretch of undivided attention on one
thought, run on a timer.

**Round** — One focused stretch within a Focus session (Pomodoro-style), followed
by a short break.

**Drift / adrift** — Time during a focus round when you left the tab and later said
it wasn't part of the task. Subtracted from your "true focus" time.

**True focus** — Your real focused minutes in a session, after subtracting time you
drifted away.

**Sweep my head** — The feature that takes one messy freeform brain-dump and sorts
it into tidy, categorised thoughts automatically.

**Plan my day** — The feature that names the two or three things that matter today
and what you can safely park.

**Ask Headroom** — A chat box (on the Overview) that can see your whole list and
answer questions about it.

**The pill** — A small always-on-top pop-out window for capturing a thought without
returning to the main site.

**The briefing** — The optional welcome panel (opened with the sun button) with a
greeting, your key numbers, and a place to ask a question.

**Overview** — The dashboard screen: your mental state and day at a glance.

**Claude** — The AI (made by Anthropic) that powers the optional helpers.

**Vercel** — The service that hosts the live website.

**GitHub** — Where the project's code is stored online.
