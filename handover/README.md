## The one-liner

Headroom turns the noise in your head into something you can *see*. You type out
whatever's circling — tasks, worries, ideas — and each one becomes a glowing point
of light on a picture of a brain. The app then helps you set things down, focus on
one thing at a time, and watch your mental load lighten over the day.

## The 30-second version

Headroom has **three screens**:

1. **Brain dump** — the home. A living brain fills the screen. You empty your head
   into it; each thought becomes a neuron.
2. **Focus** — a timer for doing one thing properly, with breaks built in. When
   you finish, it tells you honestly how the time actually went.
3. **Overview** — your day at a glance: how clear your head is, what needs
   attention, and how it's all trending.

A built-in AI coach (powered by Claude) helps you reframe worries, break big tasks
into steps, and plan your day — but the app works perfectly without it too.
