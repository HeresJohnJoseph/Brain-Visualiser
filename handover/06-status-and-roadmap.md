# 06 — Status & Roadmap

An honest picture of where Headroom is today, so nothing surprises you.

---

## What's live and working

The full experience described in this pack is **built and live** on the site:

- The Brain dump home — capture, the five categories, urgency and size, the living
  brain, opening thoughts, and the AI "set it down" / "break it down" helpers.
- Sweep my head, Plan my day, the morning briefing, and the pop-out pill.
- The Coach — overload nudges, the two-minute breathing reset, and felt ratings.
- The Focus screen — Pomodoro rounds with breaks, and the honest drift tracking
  (true focus vs. time adrift).
- The Overview — greeting, glance cards, Ask Headroom, the 14-day trend line, plus
  the to-do, habit, and quiet-session panels.
- History that quietly records each day so the trends fill in over time.

## Recent direction

The product has been through several rounds of design refinement toward a calmer,
more premium feel — inspired in part by a focus-tracking app called **Rize**, whose
strengths (a clean at-a-glance dashboard, gentle break prompts, honest session
recaps) informed Headroom's own versions, always adapted to Headroom's calmer,
privacy-first character.

A "Connections" screen (a graph showing how thoughts related to each other) was
**removed** to make room for the Focus screen, on the view that helping people
*act* on one thing beats showing them a web of everything. It can be brought back
if ever wanted.

## Known rough edges (minor, honest)

- **The name is currently fixed to "John."** The greeting says "Morning, John."
  Fine while it's a personal tool; if it's ever shared with others, a one-time
  "what should we call you?" step is the fix.
- **Notes don't sync across devices.** Because everything is stored privately in
  your browser (see [04](04-how-its-built.md)), your thoughts live on the device
  you used. Making them follow you across devices is a deliberate future decision,
  not an oversight.

## Natural next steps (not yet built)

These are candidates, not commitments:

- **Accounts and cross-device sync**, if the tool is to be shared or used across a
  laptop and phone.
- **A personalised name/onboarding** on first visit.
- **A weekly wind-down summary** — an end-of-week recap of what was cleared and how
  the week felt.
- **A companion browser extension** — the only legitimate way to get the deeper,
  Rize-style "which sites distracted me" detail, if that's ever wanted.
